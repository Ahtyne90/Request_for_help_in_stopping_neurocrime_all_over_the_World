Support for Victims of Predictive Microimplant Technology

Collecting evidence and connecting specialists worldwide so that victims can receive protection and assistance.

What is the problem?

Some technologies allow secret manipulation of memories and emotions through microimplants in the brain. These systems operate in a closed-loop mode – implants send data to the cloud, where predictive algorithms (LSTM, RNN, CNN, and others) decode hippocampal activity and can deliver stimulation on demand, influencing memory and emotional responses.

This technology, in scientific literature, is known as Ca²⁺ imaging, enabled by modern methods such as:

  • NIRS (Near-Infrared Spectroscopy) – measures neuron activity via light signal changes
  • Doppler Focused Ultrasound – precise stimulation and readout of signals in specific brain regions

The implant is a microscopic chip that transmits data to the cloud, allowing remote control in a feedback loop. From a technological standpoint, it is potentially possible to build such systems using current or near-future methods of neural recording and stimulation. This does not mean that any specific implementation has been independently verified, but the principles are within the realm of modern neuroscience and engineering.

Unfortunately, many of these experiments are conducted illegally – in some psychiatric clinics and medical university settings worldwide, often without full patient consent. Victims often do not know how to seek help and are treated as mentally ill.

Why We Need Your Help

To gather evidence for local prosecutors and human rights organizations, measurements and observations are essential:

  • Electromagnetic and NIR/ultrasound studies
  • Monitoring brain activity in controlled environments (e.g., Faraday cages or shielded safe zones)
Without these data, victims remain defenseless, and the practice is international and difficult to prove.
