Guidelines for Victims: How to Find the Right Specialists

If you suspect the presence of unauthorized micro- or nanochips operating in the 1–5 GHz range on or inside your body, it is essential to seek professional measurements and documentation.

The following types of specialists and laboratories are most relevant for detecting and analyzing such signals:

  • Radio Engineering and Telecommunications Engineers – experts in measuring radio, GSM, Wi-Fi, 5G, and radar signals using spectrum analyzers and antenna systems.
  • EMC/EMI Specialists (Electromagnetic Compatibility & Interference) – perform tests in anechoic (non-reflective) chambers to analyze electromagnetic emissions and susceptibility.
  • RF (Radio Frequency) Engineers – focus on frequencies from tens of kHz up to hundreds of GHz, including RFID, satellite, and radar technologies.
  • Electromagnetics Physicists and Engineers – model and measure fields at micro- and nano-scales.
  • Radiolocation and Radar Experts – specialize in emitting and detecting radio waves.
  • Biomedical Engineers (Neurotechnology, MedTech) – measure interactions between electromagnetic waves and the human body, including EEG, MEG, and electromagnetic stimulation.
  • Electromagnetic Field Metrology Laboratories – state or private facilities that certify and measure radiation of devices (SAR, EIRP).
  • Near-Infrared (NIR) & Optoelectronics Specialists – for cases where near-infrared fluorescence or light-based control is suspected.

Performing such measurements is absolutely necessary! Always request measurements in certified laboratories with anechoic chambers whenever possible.

In addition to anechoic chambers, measurements can also be conducted using:

  • Field measurements with spectrum analyzers and directional antennas.
  • SAR (Specific Absorption Rate) analysis – often used for IoT devices and mobile phones.
  • Interferometry and advanced electromagnetic field scanners.
  • NIR/NIR-II fluorescence detection – if light-based or optical signaling is suspected.

Potential facilities include: telecommunication research institutes, universities (electronics, telecommunication, mechatronics departments), state metrology institutes, and certification laboratories (CE, FCC testing centers).
